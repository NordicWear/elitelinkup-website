Section 3: The Lesson - Potential Doesn’t Pay Rent That whole phase burned one truth into my head: potential doesn’t pay rent. Promises don’t buy food. It doesn’t matter how close the deal sounds. Doesn’t matter how excited they seem. If the money hasn’t landed, you’re still broke. People will hype you up, promise to “send it tomorrow,” talk about “once the invoice is approved,” or “once the investor signs.” All noise. None of it feeds you. Potential is the most expensive drug in the game - it keeps you high on what might happen, while you starve on what isn’t. Now, I treat every deal the same: no emotion, no celebration, no trust - just transaction. If the money hits, cool. If not, next.

None of it means anything until the money lands. People love to talk big. They’ll tell you, “It’s coming soon,” or “The deal’s basically done.” They’ll send you screenshots, invoices, even PDFs with their logo on it - all fake comfort. It’s not real until the money is sitting in your bank account. Period. Everything else is a fantasy people tell themselves so they don’t have to face the truth: talk is cheap.

That’s when I realized how dangerous imagination can be when it’s mixed with desperation. “Soon” is where most people die - stuck waiting for what never arrives.
